It's a love letter to the city, capturing the thrill of getting lost in its streets, uncovering hidden details, and finding beauty in overlooked, dusty corners.

COSTELLAZIONE PARALLELA, 20th century Italian poetesses

Costellazione Parallela, edited by Isabella Leardini and published by Vallecchi Editore, brings to light the voices of 20th-century Italian poetesses who were long overlooked, marginalised, or excluded from the literary canon.
This book revives these influential yet forgotten figures, revealing how their voices have shaped, inspired, and reflected the inner lives of women of the 20th century and beyond.

For this project I collaborated with some of my classmates to create the illustrations.

PEN PLOTTING PARTY WORKSHOPS

Pen Plotting Party is a Rotterdam-based collective I’m part of, forged in the dynamic environment of Experimental Publishing at the Piet Zwart Institute. With backgrounds in graphic design, computing science, bookmaking, and mathematic, we bonded over pen plotters, the home office printer's predecessor lost to history. This 'obsolete' technology embodies a dynamic between new and old media, between rapid consumption and slow-paced, seamful performance.

We have hosted a(n ongoing) series of events, building community around the multiplicity of methods pen plotters invite. We started with mini-workshops to let people know and experiment with this lost technology in our favourite format: the Pen Plotting Party, where people are invited to play, fix, dance with the plotters.

LOADING: feminist server...

A card-browser game!
Get a card, type the command in the browser. Receive an interpretation of its function, a connected feminist concept and instructions to execute. Serve and be served as you turn into a feminist server yourself by playing and interacting with the cards both online or one-on-one in the space.

Through self-reflective and relational qualities, it calls upon the feminist method and literature. A reconsideration of words with a set meaning will occur while playing. The player(s) will get acquainted with important computational terms and commands, which enable associations extending beyond the terminal, into the social and eventually the personal spheres.

This is a project part of the XPUB Special Issue 23

WHAT BURNING DATA CENTER ARE YOU? A personality test

This is a personal project concerning fires in data centers, in a different way tho, to explore the mysteries of our data, how it’s stored, how surprisingly vulnerable it is to physical threats like fire, and notable data center accidents around the world.
The research takes the form of a personality test: Which Burned Data Center Are You?

The research was presented in Varia, Rotterdam, with another great side project (collective lunch)
